Ebonyi State Nigeria

Ebonyi State, located in southeastern Nigeria, is known as the "Salt of the Nation" for its rich salt deposits in areas like Okposi and Uburu. Created in 1996 from parts of Enugu and Abia states, its capital, Abakaliki, serves as the administrative and commercial hub. Renowned for agriculture, Ebonyi is a major producer of rice, yams, and cassava, earning it a place as one of Nigeria's food baskets. The state boasts a rich cultural heritage with vibrant Igbo traditions, dances, and festivals. Rapid development in infrastructure, education, and healthcare highlights its growing economic and cultural significance.
